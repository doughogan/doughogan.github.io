There's a place off Ocean Avenue...

...The Top 100 Singles of 2004

Here we have the traditional list, the songs that were on the radio and TV, the top 100 singles of the year...

This is the list with a little bit of a method to it. All of those weekly lists that I post get tallied up over the course of the year. The result, with ties broken, is what you see here. The last column below tells where the song peaked during any given week.

It's an even year, so I guess that's why I'm recycling the format... again, commentary up here...

  • The lead "Ocean Avenue" has over the rest is astonishing. It debuted on the top 30 during the first week of 2004 and spent every single week of the year in the top 30. That shattered the previous record for most weeks on the chart. With all those weekly ranks added it, it had a 41% lead over the #2 song. On top of that, if we look at play counts on my iPod (which didn't factor in here, but certainly say something), it was the most-played song. Those play counts go back to roughly the beginning of the year and tell me that I've listened to "Ocean Avenue" over 250 times on the iPod alone, 90 more than "Collide," the runner-up on the iPod. For the times when I had my car, we could add plenty more to that...
  • Speaking of runners-up... "The Reason," which lands at #2 here (and #3 on the iPod counts), has also had quite a chart life. It's been around for 40 weeks. So has "Collide," now in its 42nd week and still goin' strong. "Collide" peaked at #4, where it finished the year, making it the highest ranking song that was never #1 in any given week.
  • 2004 saw 27 different songs reach #1. "Ocean Avenue" spent nine weeks, followed by five-week runs from "I Saw" and "Somebody Told Me," followed by a four-week run from "Vertigo." Sarah McLachlan was the only artist to score three different #1 songs during the calendar year 2004 (one week each: one in January, one in June, and one in December). Meanwhile, Yellowcard had two #1 singles this year and spent a total of 11 weeks at the top of the chart.
