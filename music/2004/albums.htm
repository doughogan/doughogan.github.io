Uno, dos, tres, CATORCE...

...The Top 10 CDs of 2004

If you're surprised at what lands at the top of my year, you don't know me. It's fitting that Yellowcard should have the #1 album of the year. It spawned three #1 singles: "Way Away," "Ocean Avenue," and "Only One," but those aren't the only good ones on the album. Really, everything's quite good. Nevermind that it was released last year; it was undoubtedly the album I've listened to the most this year. In addition to the Ocean Avenue disc being great, I saw the band do two great live shows this year. The Beyond Ocean Avenue: Live at the Electric Factory, recorded the day after one of those shows, is also definitely worth checking out.

There were a lot of albums on here that were released last year, there are a few I discovered after the fact, and then there are four CDs I waited to buy right when they were released. Let's go through those, in order...

New Found Glory has been one of those bands that always gets associated with the bands I like, but they failed to put out a song I really got into until this year. When I first heard "All Downhill From Here," I honestly expected it to fade away like the rest. Something about it got it stuck in my head when I was listening to it in the car in May. Add to that the fact that that really overpriced music store in the Altoona mall was playing the whole CD a few days early when I spent a good chunk of time there one afternoon. That advertising worked, except I had to wait a while to be able to buy it... and then it became one of the things I didn't stop listening to all summer.

Next up, we have a late June release. It was the Yellowcard news page that first informed me of the Spider-Man 2 soundtrack. There were too many good names on there to pass it up... and it turned out to be a good compilation throughout. Nevermind that so few of the songs actually got played in the movie... but that's a separate rant from months ago...

Green Day's September return got a lot of press. I kept hearing about this concept of a "punk rock opera," and I'm still not sure I get it, but it's got something to do with the politcs on the album. The critics seem to think this was the best Green Day album; I like nimrod and Warning better, but it definitely has its place in the 2004 recap. (You're hearing the best song on the radio right now though.)

Then we have what has to be the biggest marketing hype ever for a CD's release, and I can't say I minded. U2's 14th album was slated for release on November 23 (my birthday, by the way) and that was preceded by months of "Vertigo"-themed iPod ads. Multiple radio stations were spinning the whole disc before its release (and I still hear "City of Blinding Lights" getting airplay despite it not being an official single) and I could tell it was going to be good. A month later, I've decided it's the best U2 album I've heard. It might not quite have anything like the gems of the '80s that made the band big, but it's the strongest CD throughout.

Enough of my rambling, onward to...
