Swing, swing swing from the tangles of my heart...

...The Top 100 Singles of 2003

Here we have the traditional list, the songs that were on the radio and TV, the top 100 singles of the year. Like usual, my list has some overlap with all those ones put out by Billboard, Radio and Records, MTV, and VH-1, and, as usual, there's some great stuff that they neglected.

This is the list with a little bit of a method to it. All of those weekly lists that I post in my AIM profile or subprofile or wherever get tallied up over the course of the year. The result, with ties broken, is what you see here. The last column below tells where the song peaked during any given week.

No comments within this time. Instead, a few observations here, starting off with follow-ups to last year's commentary:

  • Last year, I'd noted that "The Middle" dethroned "Name" for the longest run on the weekly top 30 lists. This year, "I Woke up in a Car" broke that record again.
  • Speaking of songs that came out in 2002 and whose lifetime was split across two years... there are always a few of those that that get ripped off and don't place so well in either year. "I'm With You" is the most recent inductee into that category (#41 this year, #44 last year).
  • This year's "Hey Girl" is slightly different that last's year's "Hey Girl" and that one's slightly different from the one O.A.R. originally did in 1999. The band apparantely likes it -- the third try lands the band the biggest success yet.
  • New design, no reason to milk "Yellow" for a fourth year... oh wait, I guess I just mentioned it...
  • Re: last year's "Unwell" comment -- apparantely everyone else loved it too. Radio killed it, but it's still a great song...
  • There's not really one artist who dominates this list, although it seems Coldplay had a successful year judging from its two entries in the top 5.
  • Kristi's 72-point font "swing swing" IMs didn't hurt in getting the song to #1 back in July. Then it stayed there.
  • Maroon 5 impressed me opening for Matchbox 20 in May. "Harder to Breathe" then lasted long enough to outshine all of the Matchbox 20 singles this year.
  • Yellowcard's Crowbar show is still the best live performance I saw this year. Something Corporate is the runner-up and needs to headline a show in State College!
  • Better Than Ezra is overdue for a new album.
