CS 100 Lab 7: Improved GPA Calculation

We'll build on what you did in Lab 5 here. Open your spreadsheet from that lab.

Somewhere out of the way from your existing content, add a group of cells that looks like this:

Letter Quality Points
A 4
B 3
C 2
D 1
F 0

In your previous GPA spreadsheet, you had to enter the quality points for each grade by hand. Replace what was in those cells with a formula that automatically calculates the quality points using the table given.

Then, somewhere below the GPA, program a formula that prints one of the following messages appropriately: (We'll define Dean's list as a GPA of at least 3.50 and a B average as being at least 3.0.)

  • "Congratulations, you made the Dean's List!"
  • "Congratulations, you earned at least a B GPA!"
  • "Have a good break!" (for when neither congratulatory message is appropriate.)

Finally, add a column to the right of the last data column with heading "Percentage of Total Credit Load" and for each course, calculate this value by dividing the number of credits for each course by the total number of credits. Program one formula that you fill down through the column; do not program a different formula for each row. One of the values you use changes for each row and one stays the same.

Add two more courses and grades and see the effects of changing the data without needing to change formulas.
