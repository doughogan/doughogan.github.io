CS 100 Lab 1: Basic HTML

This lab is designed to be all about practicing the content of Friday's lecture. As such, you'll put together a short page.

Follow these instructions:

  1. Create a directory called cs100 on your lab computer where you can store your work.
  2. Create a new HTML file in Notepad and save it as index.html in that directory.
  3. Set up the basic framework of your page. Make it so that the page's title reads something like "(your name)'s CS 100 Home Page."
  4. Have your name be the top-level heading on your page.
  5. Beneath that, have three sections, each at the same level of hierarchy, with titles and a paragraph beneath each:
    • My Background -- Write a paragraph telling where you're from and some biographical information about what you did before you came to UIC.
    • What I'm Doing At UIC -- Write a paragraph telling what your major is, why you chose it, what your goals are, etc.
    • What I've Learned In CS 100 So Far -- Here, just give a one-sentence paragraph saying you're taking CS 100 and have learned conceptual knowledge and some skills. Read on...
  6. Open your web page in a web browser and check that everything is working okay.
  7. Under the last section, give two lower-level headings: "Conceptual Information" and "Technical Skills." Write a paragraph beneath each, wherein you have at least one sentence for each day of class except the first.
  8. Put a horizontal line at the bottom of your page. Beneath it, put your section number on the first line and then the text "Last modified 01/25/2016." Don't put each of these things in its own paragraph; there should be no blank vertical space between these lines.
  9. Of course, check your page in a browser as you edit.

Use the TA for help as you go. Listen for TA announcements; make sure you get credit for completing the lab.
