CS 100 Lab 3: Text and Images

The goal of this lab is to put into practice ideas from last Friday's lecture and continue using previous skills.

Complete these tasks:

  1. Align Text: Go to your home page (index.html). Take the text at bottom before the horizontal rule and make it right-aligned.
  2. Add Color: Set the background color of you home page to be something other than white. (Select so that the text is readable or change the default text color too.) Furthermore, make the text at the bottom of your page below the horizontal rule be in a different color from the rest.
  3. Add A New Section: At the bottom of you home page, create a new section called "A Typical Day" with a sensible heading size. In this section, start with a paragraph where you say "My typical day starts like this:" and follow it with a numbered list of at least 2 things you do in the morning. Then have another paragraph along the lines of "Once I arrive to campus, then I do these things." Below that, have another numbered list, where the numbering picks up where you left off, and you list at least three more things.
  4. Add an Image: You have two choices here:
    • Put a picture of yourself, reasonably sized, at the top of your home page. (I figure most of you have phones with cameras and can email yourself a photo, or get a classmate to take your picture now.)
    • Add a computer-generated figure: Either use Word's Equation Editor to generate some equation, explore Word's or PowerPoint's drawing features to draw something simple, or draw something simple using another application. Take a screen shot where you capture only the relevant content, and add this to your home page in a way that makes sense.

Use the TA for help as you go. Listen for TA announcements; make sure you get credit for completing the lab.

If you've completed the lab as above (and only if the TA has told you you've done so satisfactorily to get a check for the day), you may opt to do both of the following, additionally, to go for a check-plus:

  1. Complete whichever image option above in #4 you didn't choose too.
  2. Add a small outline about something to a new section of your home page (or a separate page if that makes more sense) by nesting lists within lists. You must have three levels with two items (or more) each. You must have the top level hierarchy use Roman numerals, e.g. "I" and "II." Your second level must use capital letters, e.g. "A" and "B." You third level must use Arabic numerals, e.g. "1" and "2."
