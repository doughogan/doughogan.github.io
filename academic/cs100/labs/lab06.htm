Background

For this assignment, we'll work with an application from physics, specifically optics. Here's a little background:

When light crosses through the boundary between two materials, it bends, a phenomenon known as refraction. The index of refraction, n, of a material is a measure of its optical density, i.e. how much the material distorts the speed of light traveling through it.

Snell's Law describes the nature of the refraction in terms of the following equation: n1sin θ1 = n2sin θ2, where n1 is the index of refraction for the first material and θ1 gives the angle between a line normal to the boundary between materials 1 and 2. Similarly, n2 is the index of refraction of the second material and θ2 is the angle at which the light travels through the second material. Here is an illustration:

We'll work with some hypothetical data for five trials of a Snell's Law experiment where we're given two indices of refraction and the initial angle and we'll solve for the second angle.
